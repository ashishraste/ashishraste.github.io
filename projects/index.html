Projects

NUSwan

I am currently working on the command and control system of NUSwan (pronounced as new-swan), a team of autonomous surface vehicle (ASV) platform for monitoring freshwater bodies in Singapore. These robotic swans measure and send various physical and biological parameters of water to a central cloud storage in real-time. Thus a cooperative team provides both temporal and spatial water sampling capability. This helps us to keep track of water quality parameters such as harmful algal blooms (HABs) that affects the health of a water body's fauna.

Bumblebee

I had a great time working with the Bumblebee autonomous underwater vehicle team, during my Masters at the National University of Singapore. Our team was aware of the murky waters of the Transdec pool, where the Robosub competition is held annually drawing many enthusiastic teams from around the world. We were excited to use an imaging sonar in order to have a better vision capability supplementing the camera vision of the vehicle. As an intial step of cleaning the sonar data, we applied a mixture of spatial noise filters like Gaussian low-pass filter, temporal persistence filter, and a normalized power-law filter. These filters came in handy to eliminate the noise, to an extent, caused due to surface and bottom reflections of the pool where we conducted our tests. We were able to recognize objects placed few tens of metres away from the vehicle in terms of their range and bearing values with respect to the vehicle's position. We benefited a lot from this cool sensor which also played a major role in fetching us the runner-up position at the Robosub competition in 2015.
